Sir M Visvesvaraya

Greatest Civil Engineer of India

Here's timeline of Sir M Visvesvaraya's life :

  • 1860 - Mokshagundam Visvesvaraya was born into a Telugu Brahmin family in a Muddenahalli, Chikkaballapura. His father was a prominent Sanskrit scholar of his times. His parents were very simple but principled people.Even though the family was not rich, his parents wanted their son to get good education. He completed his primary education from his village school and went to high school in Bangalore
  • 1875 - His father died when he was just 15 and the family was plunged into poverty. In order to continue his education Visvesvaraya started giving tuitions to small children and earned his livelihood this way
  • 1881 - Completed his Bachelor of Arts from Central College,Bangalore
  • 1884 - After graduating from College Of Engineering,Pune he found a job with the Public Works Department (PWD) of Mumbai and joined as an Assistant Engineer. Over the course of this job he served in Nasik, Khandesh and Pune
  • 1895 - He designed and carried out the waterworks for the Municipality of Sukkur.He is credited with the development of the Block System which would prevent the wasteful flow of water in dams
  • 1903 - He implemented an intricate system of irrigation in the Deccan Plateau, and designed and patented a system of automatic weir water floodgates that were first installed in 1903 at Khadakvasla Reservoir near Pune
  • 1906 - His work was becoming so popular that the Government of India sent him to Aden to study water supply and drainage system.He did so and designed a project based on his study which was implemented in Aden
  • 1909 - Visvesvaraya achieved celebrity status when he designed a flood protection system for city of Hyderabad. He was instrumental in developing a system to protect Visakhapatnam port from sea erosion.This dam created the biggest reservoir in Asia when it was built
  • 1912 - He was appointed as the Diwan of Mysore.He held a position for seven years. As the Diwan, he made immense contribution towards the overall development of the state
  • 1915 - Visvesvaraya was appointed a Companion of the Order of the Indian Empire (CIE) in 1911.In 1915, while he was Diwan of Mysore, Visvesvaraya was knighted as a Knight Commander of the Order of the Indian Empire (KCIE) by the British for his contributions to the public good
  • 1917 - He helped in founding of the Government Engineering College at Bangalore ,which was later renamed as the Visvesvaraya College of Engineering in his honor
  • 1923 - He was the president of the 1923 session of the Indian Science Congress
  • 1924 - He served as the chief engineer for the construction of the Krishna Raja Sagara Lake and dam across river Kaveri in Mandya district near Mysore in Karnataka
  • 1927 - Member of the Governing Council of Tata Iron and Steel Company from 1927-1955
  • 1955 - Awarded the nation's highest honour, the Bharat Ratna
  • He was also awarded honorary membership of London Institution of Civil Engineers, a fellowship from the Indian Institute of Science (Bangalore), and several honorary degrees including D.Sc., LL.D., D.Litt. from eight universities in India
  • 1962 - Died at the age of 101
  • The Engineering Community across India celebrates Engineers Day on 15 September every year as a tribute to the greatest Indian Engineer Bharat Ratna Mokshagundam Visvesvaraya
